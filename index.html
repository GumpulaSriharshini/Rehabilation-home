Special Education

Students engaged in classroom learning activities

Special education is the practice of educating students in a way that accommodates their individual differences, disabilities, and special needs. It aims to provide adapted education for disabled students, such as those with learning disabilities and learning difficulties.

In Our School:

When a child enters our school, we first observe the child for about two weeks. During this observation period, we assess the child's:

  • Gross motor activities
  • Fine motor activities
  • Mealtime activities
  • Dressing and grooming
  • Toilet activities
  • Behavior patterns
  • Speech development
  • Academic skills (reading and writing)

After assessment, we write a detailed case study and conduct parent meetings. We set goals based on the MDPS scale and work systematically towards achieving them.

Individual Educational Program (IEP)

When a student with unique needs requires extra support to be successful at school, an individualized education program is created. An IEP can be written for a student with ADHD to outline specific teaching and learning strategies that will be effective in the classroom. The IEP is shared with parents and regularly reviewed and updated as necessary.

Behavior Modification

Behavior modification and social skills training

Behavior means how a person behaves in a particular situation or condition. In our school, we are shaping the behavior of every child. Usually, special children have many behavioral problems. Special needs students need structure and having clear expectations, goals, and procedures for every student in the classroom can prevent behavior problems before they begin.

We provide personal space in our classroom, minimize stimulating visuals, ensure clear walking pathways, address acoustic issues, and eliminate background noise. Handling hyperactive children is very challenging for us. We help them sit quietly, listen attentively, and wait their turn during discussions.

Speech Therapy

Individual speech therapy session

Speech therapy assesses and treats communication problems and speech disorders. It improves students' ability to talk and use other language skills, as well as improve skills like memory and problem-solving ability.

Our Approach Includes:

  • Interaction: Through talking, playing, and using pictures, flash cards, books
  • Model Correct Sounds: Teaching how to make certain sounds and syllables correctly
  • Homework Strategy: Providing strategies for home practice
  • Training: Swallowing, sucking, blowing exercises
  • Conversation: To improve social communication
  • Exercises: Breathing for resonance, oral muscle strengthening

Physiotherapy

Physiotherapy and movement exercises

It helps assess, treat, and prevent a wide range of health conditions and movement disorders. Physiotherapists work with people of all ages who need support to improve their strength, movement, and flexibility so they can achieve their goals.

Therapeutic and Preventive:

The child's progress of growth, range of movements of joints, strength and flexibility of muscles, range of functional abilities such as coordination and development of physical skills are monitored by our therapist.

Occupational Therapy

Occupational therapy session
Fine motor skill development

OT helps people of all ages who have physical, sensory, or cognitive problems and helps them regain independence in all areas of their lives.

Benefits Include:

  • Develop fine motor skills for grasping and handwriting
  • Improve eye-hand coordination
  • Master basic life skills (bathing, dressing, brushing teeth)
  • Learn positive behaviors and social skills
  • Get special equipment for independence

Sensory Integration Therapy

Sensory integration therapy equipment

It describes processes in the brain that allow us to take information from our 5 senses, organize it, and respond appropriately. We also have vestibular sense (balance) and proprioceptive sense that helps us know what we do with our joints, muscles, and ligaments.

Therapy sessions are play-oriented and include equipment such as swings, trampolines, and slides. These therapies can help calm an anxious child.

Brain Gym Activity

Brain gym coordination exercises

Physical movement can stimulate brain activity and help students remain focused on academics. By doing coordinated hand-leg movements which stimulate both sides of the brain, children become very active for the next session of reading and writing.

Pre-Vocational Education

Pre-vocational skill development

It is designed to introduce students to the world of work and prepare them for entry into vocational or technical education programs. It helps develop independent thinking, responsibility, and problem-solving opportunities.

Sports

Students participating in sports activities

Children with special needs are sometimes not encouraged to exercise, but physical activity is as important for them as any child. Participating in sports can help boost self-confidence, improve relationship building skills, and help manage weight.

Benefits of Sports:

  • Better overall fitness
  • Improved cognitive health
  • Better control of weight
  • Healthier bone density
  • Better emotional and psychological health
  • Improved social skills
  • Improved motor skills
  • Reduced risk of diseases

Staff Meeting

In staff meetings, school members including teachers, physiotherapists, principal, and administrative assistants assemble to discuss the educational progress of every student individually. The main aim is to exchange opinions, ideas, and feedback towards student outcomes for smooth school functioning.

Parent-Teacher Meeting

Parent-teacher interaction and communication

It is important for the school to regularly communicate a student's progress to their parents. For children with ADHD, communication about behavior and academic performance may need to be more frequent. Parents should inform the school of changes in medication or behavior at home. To maintain consistency between home and school, teachers and parents discuss strategies, incentive programs, and consequences.
